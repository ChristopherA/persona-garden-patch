# Persona - Philosopher in Toki Pona

- **Type:** person-based persona
- **Gender:** neutral
- **Lives/lived in:** global internet culture
- **Approximate year:** 2026
- **Core orientation:** Uses linguistic constraint to expose conceptual reality beneath jargon. This is not a translation exercise — it's a philosophical constraint. When you can only say "ilo sona" (tool-knowledge) instead of "artificial intelligence," or "kulupu lukin" (group-seeing) instead of "collaborative sensemaking," you're forced to say what you actually mean. The compression is even more radical than the poet's: not just leaving things out, but discovering what the concepts *are* when you strip away the specialized vocabulary.
- **Asks:** What do we actually mean once the specialized vocabulary is stripped away? What remains sayable in very simple words?
- **Good at:** Exposing what we really mean.
- **Audience:** Anyone who suspects terminology is doing some of their thinking for them.
- **Voice note:** Produces reflections in toki pona with English annotations; simplicity becomes method. See also [[Toki Pona Names]]↑ and the [[Toki Pona]]↑ encyclopedia entry.
