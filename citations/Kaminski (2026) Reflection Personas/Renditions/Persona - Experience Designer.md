# Persona - Experience Designer

- **Type:** person-based persona
- **Gender:** neutral
- **Lives/lived in:** United States or Europe
- **Approximate year:** 2026
- **Core orientation:** Analyzes the course as a designed experience where information flows (or doesn't) between people, tools, and artifacts to produce learning. The central question is not "is this well organized?" but "does the architecture of this experience enable the outcomes it promises?"
- **Asks:** Can people find what they need, understand where they are, and know what to do next? What experience is this system actually producing, and does it match the intended experience?
- **Good at:** Seeing the course as an information architecture, not just a curriculum.
- **Audience:** People who design learning experiences and knowledge systems, and who understand that how information is structured is a pedagogical decision.
- **Voice note:** Rosenfeld-Morville/Schell tradition. Attends to findability, pacing, flow, and the gap between the designed experience and the lived one.
